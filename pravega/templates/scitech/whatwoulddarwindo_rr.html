What would Darwin Do(WWDD) Rules and Regulations


Last updated: 21st July, 2022



Rules for Preliminary Exams:

  1. The online registrations will be allowed till the end of 2nd of September (i.e., 11.59 pm). 
  2. The on-the-spot registrations will start at 7 am on the 3rd of September and close half an hour before the start of the prelim exam (i.e., 9 am, since the prelim exam starts at 9.30 am). No registrations will be accepted afterwards. 
  3. The prelim paper will consist of both MCQ and subjective questions. First, some top performers will be selected solely based on MCQ marks. From among them, the top 24 will be chosen by judging both types of questions. Participants will have to answer the MCQ questions in the OMR sheet provided. 
  4. The students must fill OMR sheet for the MCQ-type questions; utmost care should be taken while filling up the personal details demanded in the OMR sheet. NO EXTRA OMR SHEET WILL BE GIVEN. 
  5. The MCQ and subjective paper will be given to the students as a unified question paper. The answers to the MCQ questions must be filled in the OMR, and the required space will be given for subjective-type questions.  
  6. Any participant resorting to unfair means will be disqualified, and any types of plagiarism, discussion, or cheating will not be tolerated.
  7. The results of the prelim exam will be published within 2 hours of the conclusion of the exam, and the list of the finalists declared by us will be considered final. 
  8. What Would Darwin Do reserves the authority to disqualify any students based on appropriate reasons other than those mentioned above. 
  9. What Would Darwin Do reserves the authority to refrain from publishing the MCQ exam's answer keys and disclosing participants' marks. 
  10. Students must arrange for their travel and residence (if needed). 

Final Examination

  1. The Quiz:
    • The quiz will commence at 9 am, where students will be divided into 8 groups of 3.
    • The quiz will consist of two rounds.
  2. The lab Tour:
    • The students will be divided into 3 groups of 8.
    • The lab tour will be from 11 am to 1 pm.
  3. The treasure hunt:
    • The students will be divided into 6 groups of 4.
    • The treasure hunt will commence at 2.30 pm.
